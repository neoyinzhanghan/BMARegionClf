Treatment  
Treatment of ET aims at reducing the platelet counts in  
symptomatic patients. Recently, Anegrelide , a drug that  
inhibits megakaryopoiesis, has been approved for the treat-  
ment of ET. The drug has almost replaced other and more  
toxic treatments such as hydroxyurea. The prognosis of  
patients with ET is usually very good, provided that the  
platelet counts are well controlled.

Leukocytosis and increased platelet numbers are observed in

Idiopathic Myelofibrosis  
It is estimated that 10% of patients with PV undergo  
evolution to myelofibrosis. This phase is characterized by  
increasing splenomegaly, excessive reticulin in the bone  
marrow, and a leukoerythroblastic picture in blood.  
Transformation to acute myeloid leukemia occurs in  
approximately 9% of patients with PV.  
Idiopathic myelofibrosis is a clonal myeloproliferative disor-  
der characterized by the presence of splenomegaly, leuko-  
erythroblastic blood picture, excessive teardrop erythro-  
cytes, marrow fibrosis, and extramedullary hematopoiesis.  
The hematopoietic abnormality in myelofibrosis arises from  
a defect at the stem cell level. However, it should be empha-  
sized that mesenchymal cells and fibroblasts are not part of  
the malignant clone and that marrow fibrosis is considered  
secondary or reactive. The cause of fibrosis in this disease is  
a matter of some debate. Dysregulation in the synthesis of  
collagen, excessive release of growth factors, and abnormal-  
ities of platelet activation have been implicated as possible  
sources of marrow fibrosis.  
Treatment  
Treatment of PV aims at reducing blood cell mass in symp-  
tomatic patients by phlebotomy or other therapeutic  
modalities.

Clinical Features  
The average age of patients at the time of diagnosis of ET is  
55 years. Increased platelet counts may be an incidental  
finding or patients may present with bleeding or thrombot-  
ic problems. Splenomegaly can be found in approximately  
40% of patients with ET.

myeloid precursors, nucleated red cells, and large platelets.
